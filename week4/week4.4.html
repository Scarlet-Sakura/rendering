Question - Part 1:

A 200 W spherically shaped light bulb (20% efficiency) emits red light of wavelength 650 nm equally in all directions. The light bulb is placed 2m above the table. Calculate the irradiance at the table.

Answer - Part 1:

Φ = ε*P = 0.2*200 = 40 Watt. E = Φ/A = 40/(4*pi*r^2) = 0.7957 W/m^2.

Question - Part 2:

Photometric quantities can be calculated from radiometric ones based on the equation:

Photometric = Radiometric * 685 * V(λ) (1)

In which V(λ) is the luminous efficiency curve. At 650nm, the luminous efficiency curve has value of 0.1. Calculate the illuminance.

Answer - Part 2:

From (1) the illuminance is : 0.7957 * 685 * 0.1 = 54.5 L/m^2.
